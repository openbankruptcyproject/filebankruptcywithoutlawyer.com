Bankruptcy Forms Guide

Every official form you need, where to get them for free, and the most common mistakes on each one.

Where to get the forms

All official bankruptcy forms are free at uscourts.gov/forms/bankruptcy-forms. Never pay for forms -- they are public documents. Your court may also require local forms from its website.

Do not use outdated forms. Forms are updated periodically. Always download the latest version. Filing on outdated forms can result in rejection.

Tips for completing the forms

  • Be thorough: List every asset, every debt, every income source. Omissions can deny your discharge under 11 U.S.C. § 727(a)(4).
  • Use your credit reports as a checklist: Pull all three from annualcreditreport.com and cross-reference every account.
  • Err on disclosure: When unsure whether to list something, list it. Over-disclosure is far safer than under-disclosure.
  • Double-check addresses: The court notifies creditors using your schedules. Wrong addresses mean missed notices.
  • Research your state's exemptions: Schedule C is critical. Each state has its own exemption scheme. Some allow choosing between state and federal exemptions.
  • Use the instructions: Each form has official instructions. Read them carefully before filling out the form.

Free preparation tools exist. Upsolve offers a free Chapter 7 preparation tool for income-qualifying filers. Some courts provide fillable PDFs with built-in calculations. Check your court's pro se resources page.
